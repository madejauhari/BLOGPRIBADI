My Journey

My name is Made Jauhari, the second of three siblings, raised in a family where independence and responsibility were emphasized from an early age. Growing up, I learned the importance of hard work and self-reliance, values that have guided me through both personal and academic challenges. Today, I am pursuing my passion for technology as a student at Universitas Udayana, where I’m enrolled in the Information Technology program, class of 2022. Currently in my 5th semester, I have immersed myself in the fascinating world of IT, exploring everything from programming to artificial intelligence, and constantly expanding my skill set.

The world of technology captivates me because of its limitless potential. Whether it's developing software, working on data analysis, or diving into the complexities of artificial intelligence, I thrive on the problem-solving and creativity that this field demands. Outside the classroom, I enjoy experimenting with new coding projects, staying up to date with the latest tech trends, and learning from the ever-evolving landscape of digital innovation.

In addition to my studies, I’ve always had a strong interest in exploring how technology can bridge gaps in education and business, especially in areas like artificial intelligence and digital economics. I believe that technology not only shapes our future but also has the power to create opportunities for others. That’s why I’m passionate about using my skills to make a meaningful impact, whether it’s through a simple software solution or a larger-scale project.

Looking ahead, I am determined to continue growing both academically and personally. My journey in IT is just beginning, but I’m excited for what lies ahead. Every challenge presents a new opportunity for learning and improvement, and I am committed to making the most of this journey. Fueled by a desire to push boundaries, I am eager to explore the intersection of technology and business, striving to contribute to innovations that can make a real difference in the world.
